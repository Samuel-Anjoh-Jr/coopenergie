CONFIDENTIAL — Hackathon Submission Document

1. Introduction
1.1 Purpose
This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) defines the functional and non-functional requirements for CoopEnergie — a real blockchain-enabled cooperative solar funding platform built for the Cameroonian market and the broader CEMAC zone.
The document is structured in two parts:
Part A — Hackathon MVP Requirements: The minimum deliverable set for Phases 1, 2, and 3 of MIABE Hackathon 2026.
Part B — Production Implementation Requirements: Full production features being actively built beyond the hackathon scope. These are not "future roadmap" items — they are committed implementation work.
1.2 Project Overview
CoopEnergie enables citizens to form digital cooperatives, pool solar funding contributions, vote collectively on purchases, and verify every transaction on the public Celo blockchain. The platform addresses a critical trust gap: informal cooperatives dissolve before achieving their goals because members cannot verify that funds are being managed honestly.
By recording every contribution, vote, and financial decision on the Celo blockchain, CoopEnergie makes fraud immediately visible and provable — not through internal promises, but through public on-chain verification at celoscan.io.
1.3 Problem Statement
45% of Cameroon's population lacks electricity access — a significant market for cooperative solar funding.
A basic solar system costs 60,000–250,000 FCFA — inaccessible for most rural households individually.
The Cameroonian solar market has grown 35% per year since 2018, showing strong latent demand.
~80,000 households were individually equipped with solar panels by 2023.
Cooperative purchasing in Kenya and Ethiopia reduced unit costs by 25–40% — a proven model.
Without transparent fund management, cooperative funds become conflict sources, dissolving groups before purchase.
1.4 Why Celo Blockchain for Africa
The hackathon theme "La Blockchain, levier du développement durable africain" calls for real blockchain leverage — not simulation. CoopEnergie implements smart contracts on the Celo network, which was designed from the ground up for exactly this context.

Factor
Celo Advantage
Mobile-first design
Celo was built specifically for mobile devices and low-bandwidth environments — the exact reality of African users.
Transaction fees
Gas fees below $0.001 USD per transaction — negligible for FCFA-denominated cooperatives.
User abstraction
Meta-transactions via a gas relayer: the platform pays gas on behalf of users. Users interact normally; blockchain happens invisibly.
Verification
Every transaction is publicly verifiable on celoscan.io — the cooperative wallet address is displayed in the app with a direct explorer link.
African adoption
Celo's Valora wallet and Moola Market protocol are already in active use across Sub-Saharan Africa.
Ethereum-compatible
Solidity smart contracts — auditable, industry-standard tooling (Hardhat, OpenZeppelin).
Stable currency
cUSD (Celo Dollar) available for price-stable contributions; FCFA amounts recorded on-chain.
No wallet required
Users never need to install MetaMask or hold CELO — the platform acts as a custodial relayer.

6.4 Gas Relayer Strategy
The gas relayer ensures users never need to hold CELO or understand gas fees:
User submits an action (contribution/vote) via the web or mobile app.
Backend signs the transaction using the platform relayer wallet (which holds CELO for gas).
CooperativeVault verifies the relay signature and executes the call as if it came from the user's address.
The Celo transaction appears in the user's address history on celoscan.io — fully attributable to them.
The relayer wallet is topped up automatically from platform revenue.
Cost estimate: at <$0.001 per transaction and an average cooperative generating 500 transactions/year, annual gas cost per cooperative is under $0.50 USD. Fully sustainable.

CamPay integration requires API access and sandbox credentials from CamPay.
Offline-first mobile requires Expo SDK >= 52 and stable SQLite/MMKV support.
14.2 Assumptions
Target users have smartphones and at least 2G connectivity.
Cooperative administrators have basic digital literacy.
FCFA is the primary operating currency; CELO is only used internally for gas.
Primary market: Cameroon — CEMAC zone expansion planned.
Users do not need to understand blockchain; they interact with a standard mobile/web UI.
